iInsight One

You are selling properties when you should be selling transformations

Meta's targeting is barely a problem anymore — the platform finds the right buyers automatically if your ad gives it enough signal. The real reason your ads fail isn't who you reach. It's what you say to them when you reach them.

Look at almost every property ad in your feed. They all say the same thing:

"Now selling. 4 bedroom duplex. Lekki. ₦150 Million. Call now." That's not marketing. That's a billboard pretending to be an ad.

Here's the deeper truth, proven by a marketing legend named Eugene Schwartz in 1966 and validated by every world-class copywriter since. Your buyers exist on five different levels of awareness. Most Nigerian developers run ads only to the tiny fraction ready to buy today — fighting over the same 5% of the market everyone else is fighting over.

But here's what matters most. At every level, you're not selling a property. You're selling a transformation. Nobody buys a 4-bedroom duplex. They buy:

  • → The end of paying rent every January for the rest of their life
  • → The pride of bringing their parents to see "their place"
  • → The financial security of owning land that doubles in value
  • → The legacy they can pass to their children
  • → The peace of never being told to "vacate by month-end" again
The cost of ignoring thisYou run ads that only convert the 5% ready to buy this month. The other 95% — who'd have bought from you in 30–90 days — go to your competitor instead.
iiInsight Two

Your lead form goes straight to WhatsApp with no filter

When your ad sends people directly to WhatsApp, your sales team becomes a free customer-service desk for tyre-kickers. Real buyers wait too long for replies. And you have no data on who's actually qualified.

A proper qualifying funnel asks the right questions before anyone reaches your team — budget, timeline, purpose, location, decision authority. By the time someone lands in your WhatsApp, they've already self-identified as serious. Your team only speaks to people worth speaking to.

⚡ Insider tipIn Nigerian real estate, "DM for price" kills conversion. State your price range openly. Yes, it filters people out. That is the point.
The cost of ignoring thisYour best closers spend 80% of their day on people who'll never buy. The 20% who would have bought never get the attention they deserved — and they go elsewhere.
iiiInsight Three · the one that hurts most

You're sleeping on the realtor channel — and it's killing your numbers

Most developers see realtors as a side hustle. The developers quietly dominating Nigerian real estate treat them as their primary distribution army — hundreds, sometimes thousands of independent agents promoting their properties daily.

Over 60% of home seekers search online — but they don't trust ads. They trust people. Specifically, realtors who recommend properties inside WhatsApp groups, DMs, and conversations.

The cost of ignoring thisWhile you pay ₦1,500 per lead through ads, your competitors get them free through a realtor network you don't have. They're scaling. You're spending.
