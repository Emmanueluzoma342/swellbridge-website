Act One — The Value

The real problem with most
Nigerian small business websites.

Here's something nobody told you when you started looking for a website: your website doesn't have to be fancy. It has to be clear.

Most Nigerian small business websites fail not because they look bad — but because they answer the wrong questions. They tell visitors about the business owner. Their history. Their team. Their awards.

But the visitor — your potential customer — is silently asking 3 questions:

Are these people real, or will they scam me?
Can they actually do what I need?
How do I reach them right now while I'm interested?

If your website doesn't answer all three within the first 5 seconds of scrolling — you've lost the customer. They've already gone back to Instagram, WhatsApp Status, or a competitor's page.

The fundamentals

The 3 things every
first website must have.

We promised in the ad to show you this, and we will. Here it is. Every successful business website we've built had these three things at the top.

01

A clear sentence that says what you do and who you do it for.

Not "Welcome to our website." Not "Excellence in service since 2018." A clear, specific sentence like "We design custom kitchen cabinets for homes in Lekki and Ikoyi." If a visitor can't tell what you sell within 5 seconds, they leave.

02

Proof that you're real and trustworthy.

Real photos of your work. Real customer messages (WhatsApp screenshots count). A real address or area you operate in. A real phone number that actually answers. Nigerian buyers have been burned by too many fake online businesses — your website has 5 seconds to convince them you're not one of them.

03

A big, obvious WhatsApp button.

Not just a "contact us" form. A WhatsApp button. Because in Nigeria, this is how business happens. The faster you let them message you, the more sales you close. Forms get ignored. WhatsApp gets opened.

Get these three right, and your website will outperform most small business sites in Nigeria — no matter how big or small it is.

What to avoid

The 3 most expensive
mistakes first-time buyers make.

Now that you know what to do, here's what to avoid. We've watched business owners spend ₦300k–₦1M and walk away with nothing useful. Here's why.

Mistake 01

Hiring the cheapest designer they can find.

We get it — budgets are tight. But the cheapest option in Nigeria's web design market is almost always someone who'll deliver a template, vanish after launch, or simply stop replying halfway through. You're not saving money. You're paying twice.

Mistake 02

Building a website without thinking about who it's actually for.

Your website's job is to talk to your customer, not to you. Many business owners design their site around what they want to say — their history, their awards, their team. The customer doesn't care. The customer wants to know "can you solve my problem, and how do I reach you?" Every page, every section, every word should be built around that.

Mistake 03

Treating the website as a brochure instead of a salesperson.

Your website's job isn't to just look pretty. Its job is to take a visitor from "curious" to "messaging you on WhatsApp." Every section, every image, every line should be pulling them toward that one action. If a section doesn't help convert visitors into messages, it's wasted space.
