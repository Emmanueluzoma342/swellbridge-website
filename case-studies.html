How Natweng Realtors closed a ₦40M duplex deal from a single Facebook ad campaign.

The situation: Natweng Realtors had a quality inventory of properties but was burning through Facebook ad budget without converting leads into actual sales. The leads were coming in — but most went cold within 48 hours.

Real Estate · Lagos
Engagement: 2023–2024

How we built a 2,000-realtor army for a major Lagos developer in 6 months.

The situation: A Lagos developer with serious inventory was relying entirely on direct ads to sell. Cost per acquisition was painful, scaling was difficult, and they had no leverage when Meta's algorithms shifted.

What we did: We architected a parallel realtor recruitment engine — recruitment ads, onboarding sequences, training content, a private community, and a commission system. Realtors became distributed salespeople, each selling to their own networks.

The result: Within six months, the developer had over 2,000 active realtors promoting their projects. Net result: 10 acres of land sold through realtor-driven sales alone, plus a permanent distribution moat competitors couldn't replicate.

Real Estate · Benin City
Engagement: 2023

How a Benin City developer generated hundreds of millions in sales from a new market.

The situation: A Benin-based real estate developer wanted to scale beyond word-of-mouth referrals. The Lagos-style marketing playbooks they'd seen online didn't translate to the Benin buyer — different psychology, different price sensitivity, different objections.

What we did: We built a Benin-specific campaign architecture. Local language nuances in copy, locally relevant social proof, lower-friction lead capture, and a phone-first (not WhatsApp-first) follow-up system.

The result: Hundreds of millions of Naira in property sales over the engagement period. New buyer demographics opened up. The developer became one of the most recognised names in their market.

Real Estate · Lagos
Engagement: 2024

How a solo Lagos realtor sold 3 lands from a campaign that cost less than her phone bill.

The situation: An independent realtor had a small marketing budget (under ₦100k/month) and was competing against firms spending 10x more. She needed an asymmetric edge.

What we did: We focused entirely on direct-response, highly targeted micro-campaigns aimed at very specific buyer profiles. Tight audience, sharp copy, no wasted spend. Plus a simple WhatsApp follow-up sequence she could run herself.

The result: Three lands sold within the campaign period. Her cost per closed sale was roughly ₦25,000 — a number most agencies would call impossible in Nigerian real estate.
